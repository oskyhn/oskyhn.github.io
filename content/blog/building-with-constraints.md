Some of the best engineering solutions emerge from working within tight constraints. When resources are limited, we're forced to think more carefully about what truly matters.

## The paradox of constraints

More compute doesn't always lead to better outcomes. In fact, unlimited resources can lead to lazy solutions that don't generalize well.

## Lessons from limited settings

Working with constrained compute taught me:

- The importance of data quality over quantity
- How architectural choices matter more than scale
- Why simple baselines should never be skipped

## Applying this thinking

Even when resources are available, approaching problems as if they weren't can lead to more robust solutions.
